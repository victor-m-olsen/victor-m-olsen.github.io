Food Security

Research project published in Nature Food, leveraging remote sensing, econometric and spatial analysis to reveal the impact of armed conflict on food security in South Sudan:

The impact of conflict-driven cropland abandonment on food insecurity in South Sudan revealed using satellite remote sensing

Abstract

Armed conflicts often hinder food security through cropland abandonment and restrict the collection of on-the-ground information required for targeted relief distribution. Satellite remote sensing provides a means for gathering information about disruptions during armed conflicts and assessing the food security status in conflict zones. Using ~7,500 multisource satellite images, we implemented a data-driven approach that showed a reduction in cultivated croplands in war-ravaged South Sudan by 16% from 2016 to 2018. Propensity score matching revealed a statistical relationship between cropland abandonment and armed conflicts that contributed to drastic decreases in food supply. Our analysis shows that the abandoned croplands could have supported at least a quarter of the population in the southern states of South Sudan and demonstrates that remote sensing can play a crucial role in the assessment of cropland abandonment in food-insecure regions, thereby improving the basis for timely aid provision.

Water Resources

Yemen experiences extreme water scarcity with large seasonal variations in severity. This project mapped surface water resources at 10 meter resolution in monthly intervals throughout 2020. The method is based on Sentinel-1 and -2 time-series and supervised machine learning using automatically sampled training data. To our knowledge, these are the most detailed surface water maps of Yemen and provide information about the seasonality of water resources in rivers, lakes and reservoirs. Agricultural Monitoring

Syria experiences periodic food and water scarcity. This project produced an agricultural monitoring tool that tracks cropland, surface water and precipitation at monthly intervals across Syria. Using 10 m resolution vegetation indices, semi-automatic image classification and precipitation measurements, the tool combines the key indicators of food and water scarcity, thus enabling analysis at the local scale and early action.

Urban Planning

A large number of factors must be considered when deciding on the optimal location for new infrastructure. Including long-term energy efficiency in the decision making process can be challenging. This project produced a geospatial tool to facilitate decision making in urban planning. The tool allows users to perform simple multi-criteria analysis in their GIS using existing data. In doing so, spatial parameters such as availability of central heating, distance to existing roads and cadestral data can be taken into account through user specified prioritisation. Night Light

Night light images are measurements of light emission from earthâ€™s surface, captured by earth observing satellites. Light emissions from the surface of the earth generally stem from either fire or anthropogenic light - the latter being most predominant in populated areas. Measurements of anthropogenic night light are closely correlated with electricity usage, which is a valuable indicator of the condition of infrastructure, economics and demographics. Previous studies have applied night light analysis for studying population density, poverty, urban development, detecting armed conflict, recovery patterns from natural disaster and fishing activity. Flood Hazard

Many flood prone regions still lack adequate data to mitigate flood hazard. Using HEC-RAS flood modelling software, a high resolution DEM and land cover data, this project delivered a flood model in a highly vulnerable region to support humanitarian planning.
